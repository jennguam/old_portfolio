Ann Arbor Learning Partnership


Illustrator & Photoshop CS6

Goal: In the graduate level graphic design class I took, we were given requirements and told to design an entire suite of graphic design products for a particular company: logos, logo style guides, color guides, home page layouts… the works. The professors run a real graphic design firm and the “mock” requirements were adjusted from legitimate requests they’d gotten from real clients over the years.

Iterated on logo designs based on provided production samples.

This project forced us to focus on process – it wasn’t just a one week homework assignment, we had to turn in deliverables every week for several weeks and make corrections based on feedback until we got the designs to a place that the “client” would like. And, as the client was once theirs, they’d know. (Granted, process for a graphic design project is different for the process for a UX project.)

I first worked at this in the small scale with the logo. Once that was set, it would inform the theme for the site comps. After designing 3 logos, the instructors (aka “the client”) and I settled on one to move forward with.


Created a detailed logo guide.

Logo guides are an important tool for maintaining a consistent look and feel for a brand. I feel like the one I created is pretty professional; it contains spacing guidlines, a variety of logo sizes, color and font details, and even black and white treatments.
